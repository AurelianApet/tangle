Privacy Policy

Samyang Foods Co., Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as the "Company") complies with the Personal Information Protection Act and other related laws and regulations to protect the freedom and rights of users, lawfully process personal information and manage it safely. In accordance with Article 30 of the Personal Information Protection Act, the Company establishes and discloses a privacy policy to guide the procedures and standards for processing personal information and to handle related complaints quickly and smoothly.

Article 1. Collection Items and Purpose of Use of Personal Information

The Company processes personal information for the following purposes. Processed personal information will not be used for purposes other than the following purposes, and if the purpose of use is changed, the Company will take necessary measures such as obtaining separate consent and etc. in accordance with Article 18 of the Personal Information Protection Act.

Article 2. Processing and Retention Period of Personal Information

The Company may process and retain personal information within the period of time specified by law for retaining and using personal information or within the period of time agreed upon by the user at the time of collecting the personal information and may not destroy personal information whose retention and use period has elapsed.
However, personal information whose retention period has elapsed will not be destroyed and may be kept without being destroyed in cases where it needs to be preserved according to "other laws and regulations" or where "separate consent has been obtained."

Record of consumer complaints or dispute resolution
Reason for retention: Consumer Protection Act in E-commerce, etc.

Article 3. Destruction of Personal Information

Personal information of users shall be destroyed without delay once the purpose of collecting such information has been achieved as originally notified. In addition, personal information of information subjects shall be destroyed without delay except for the data that must be retained under the provisions of related laws specified in Article 2, in the event of revocation of consent. However, the maximum period of "without delay" shall not exceed 7 days to protect the service user's rights in case of any request for restoration or recovery. In accordance with Article 21 of the Personal Information Protection Act, personal information printed on paper shall be destroyed by shredding or incineration, and personal information stored in electronic file format shall be destroyed by using a technical or physical method that cannot reproduce the record. Personal information that has been transferred to a separate DB after the processing purpose has been achieved shall be safely stored and managed in accordance with internal regulations and relevant laws and regulations and shall be destroyed without delay when the retention period has expired. In this case, personal information transferred to a separate DB shall not be used for any purpose other than the agreed purpose or beyond the scope of applicable laws and regulations.

Article 4. Entrustment of Personal Information Processing

The Company does not entrust the processing of personal information to any third party.

Article 5. Provision of Personal Information to Third Parties

The Company does not provide personal information of users to any third party except in cases where the user has given separate consent or where it is prescribed by law. Company will disclose in this Privacy Policy in the event it processes, consigns, or provides a third party with the personal information pursuant to Articles 28(2) through 28(7) of the Personal Information Protection Act. 

Article 6. Collection of Personal Information by Cookies

➀ 'Cookie' is a temporary file created automatically when accessing a website, which is a small amount of information sent by the server operating the website to the user's computer browser and is sometimes stored on the hard disk of the user's PC.
➁The Company uses 'cookies' to save and retrieve user information on a regular basis in order to provide fast and useful services when users use the website.
➂Users have the right to choose whether or not to accept cookies, and can set their web browser options to allow all cookies, ask for confirmation each time a cookie is saved, or block all cookies. However, if a user refuses to save cookies, there may be restrictions on using some services that require login information to be entered again.

- The method of designating whether to allow cookie installation (in the case of Internet Explorer) is as follows.

Article 7. Collection, Use, and Provision of Behavioral Information and Refusal of Consent

The Company uses Google Analytics, a web log analysis tool provided by Google, to provide users with better services.
Google Analytics collects behavioral information of website users through cookies. In this case, only non-personally identifiable information that cannot identify individual users is collected. However, users can refuse to use Google Analytics by installing the Google Analytics blocking browser add-on (https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout) or by denying cookies in their web browser settings.
The methods to block/allow customized advertisements through web browsers are as follows.

Internet Explorer (Internet Explorer 11 for Windows 10)
- Select the Tools button in Internet Explorer and then select Internet options.
- Select the Privacy tab and choose Advanced under Settings, then select either Block or Allow cookies

Microsoft Edge
- Click the '...' icon in the upper-right corner of the Edge window and select Settings.
- Click 'Privacy, search, and services' on the left side of the Settings page and select the level of tracking prevention you want under the 'Tracking prevention' section.
- Choose whether to always use "Strict" tracking prevention when searching InPrivate.
- Select whether to send a "Do Not Track" request under the "Privacy" section at the bottom of the page.

Chrome Browser
- Click the '⋮' icon (Customize and control Chrome) in the upper-right corner of the Chrome window and select Settings.
- Click 'Advanced' at the bottom of the Settings page, and click 'Content settings' under the 'Privacy and security' section.
- Under the 'Cookies' section, select the checkbox for 'Block other companies cookies and site data'.

Article 8 Measures for Securing the Safety of Personal Information

The company takes the following measures to secure the safety of personal information.

1. Minimization and education of personal information handling employees
The company designates employees to handle personal information and restricts them to a minimum to manage personal information effectively.

2. Establishment and implementation of internal management plan
The company establishes and implements an internal management plan to ensure the secure handling of personal information.

3. Encryption of personal information
Passwords among users' personal information are encrypted and stored and managed so that only the users themselves can know them. Additionally, the company uses separate security functions such as encrypting files and transmission data or using file locking functions for important data.
